Smoke alarms will warn you if smoke is present inside a room. It is good to have one in every room of your house in case of an actual fire. Make sure to test if it's functioning properly every month and replace the batteries once a year.
Planning your escape route for your house in case of a fire is highly advisable. Make sure to mark down paths and possible exits and meeting point after escaping the structure. Practice this plan with others inside your home, so that they know where to go in a fire.
If you smoke, it's good to have well-ventilated areas for the smoke to pass through. Make sure to always put the cigarette out completely before disposing them. And never place them on anything that may catch fire.
Never leave whatever is cooking on the stove. Always keep an eye on it. Keep items that may catch fire away from the stove or anything hot.
Keep heaters away from kids, pets, or anything flammable. Never leave them on when you're out of the house or while you're sleeping.
Keep these tools out of their reach or locked somewhere and inform children it is not a toy and is dangerous to play with.
Make sure to replace any damaged cord. If it smells funny, unplug it immediately and have a professional take a look at it. Don't tamper with a fuse if you are not knowledgable.
Water sprinklers are more than capable of putting out fire should it be present.
